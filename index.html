Introduction

I'm a mathematics student at the University of St Andrews. My experience is in computational mathematics, mathematical physics, and embedded security. Some of my other interests are random systems, computer graphics, and signal processing.

About me

From June 2024 until December 2024, I worked as an automotive cryptography engineer for NVIDIA, where I directed the fuzzing of the PKCS#11 library.

During the summer of 2023, I received a UNIQ+ research internship at the University of Oxford. I spent the summer working in the mathematical physics group with Dr Christopher Couzens on using genetic algorithms to find \(AdS_4\) solutions.

A few months earlier, during the final semester of my first year at St Andrews, I worked on a research project with Dr Dan Lucas at the University of St Andrews as part of the St Andrews Research Internship Scheme. I worked on applying statistical analysis methods to recreate the statistics of a turbulent flow from its unstable periodic orbits.

Publications

    Published in Physical Review Fluids (in press), 2024
    Ed Redfern, Andrei Lazer, Dan Lucas

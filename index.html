Welcome!

I’m a mathematics student at the University of St Andrews, pursuing a bachelor’s degree with honors.

My experiences are in computational maths, mathematical physics, and embedded security. My other interests include random systems, computer graphics, and signal processing.

About me

From June to December 2024, I worked as an automotive cryptography engineer for NVIDIA, where I directed the fuzzing of the PKCS#11 library. I used C to develop various parts of the library on QNX and embedded Linux.

During the summer of 2023, I received a UNIQ+ research internship at the University of Oxford. I spent the summer working in the mathematical physics group with Dr Christopher Couzens on using genetic algorithms to find consistent metrics in \(AdS_4\).

A few months earlier, during the final semester of my first year at St Andrews, I worked on a research project with Dr Dan Lucas at the University of St Andrews as part of the St Andrews Research Internship Scheme. I applied statistical analysis methods to recreate the statistics of a turbulent flow from its unstable periodic orbits.

Publications

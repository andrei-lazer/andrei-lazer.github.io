Predicting turbulent flows using unstable periodic orbits

(Link)

This project, which then became a publication in Physical Review Fluids titled Dynamically relevant recurrent flows obtained via a nonlinear recurrence function from two-dimensional turbulence, was my first bit of research experience, and currently my only publication.

This resulted from the StARIS internship at the University of St Andrews. My only contributions are the majority of Section IV, where I used k-means clustering, pruning, and regression techniques to predict certain quantities of a turbulent flow.

Click here for some pretty pictures generated by my co-authors, or here for some (not as pretty) plots that I created.

This was my first foray into computational maths research, and I am extremely thankful to my supervisor Dan Lucas for all of his support and mentorship. I would not be where I am now without his help.

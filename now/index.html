reading

I've just finished Butcher's Crossing, and I unsurprisingly really enjoyed it. John Williams has a talent for writing the bleakest stories I've ever read, and they resonate with me for some reason.

I've given up on The Count of Monte Cristo, since reading dense 19th century French fiction isn't particularly relaxing during my master's. Maybe I'll pick it up at some other point.

I started reading Revenge by Yoko Ogawa yesterday. I quite liked The Housekeeper and the Professor by the same author, and this book is proving to be similarly heartwrenching.
